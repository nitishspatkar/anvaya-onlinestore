The Ritual

01.

Tempering

Introduce the bar to warm water. The triple-milling process requires a few seconds of warmth to release the first layer of volatile aromatic oils.

02.

Lathering

Work into a dense, non-bubbling cream between the palms. The texture should feel more like a moisturizing balm than a traditional soap.

03.

Absorption

Massage directly onto damp skin in slow, circular motions. Focus on pulse points to allow the cooling nature of Khus to settle.

04.

Cleansing

Rinse with cool water to lock in the earthy scent. Pat dry gently to preserve the fine veil of essential oils left on the epidermis.

The Provenance

Kannauj: The
Perfume Capital

Our Khus roots are harvested from the alluvial plains of the Ganges. Using the ancient Deg-Bhapka method—a traditional steam distillation in copper stills—we capture the essence of 'Mitti' (earth) and Vetiver. This centuries-old process ensures that no synthetic stabilizers are needed, leaving the scent as pure as the soil itself.
